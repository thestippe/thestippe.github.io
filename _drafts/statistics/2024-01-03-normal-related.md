$$
p(x | \mu, \sigma) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2   \pi  \sigma^2}}e^{-\frac{\left(x-\mu\right)^2}{\sigma^2}}
$$

$$
X \sim \mathcal{N}(\mu, \sigma)
$$


The sum of $n$ normal variables is a normal variable itself.
